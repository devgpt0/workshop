# Sakshi and the Festival of Lamps - Chunk 01

In the old city of Varanasi, where temple bells greeted every sunrise, lived a kind girl named Sakshi. After her father passed away, Sakshi was left in the care of her stepmother and two stepsisters. They wore bright silks and gold bangles, while Sakshi was given faded cotton and endless chores. She cleaned brass lamps, fetched water from the ghat, and cooked meals she was not allowed to eat first. Even then, Sakshi kept her gentle smile and helped neighbors whenever she could.
